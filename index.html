Jesus in the Eucharist

Catholics, those that are truly Catholic anyway, believe Jesus when he said in John Chapter 6: "Amen, Amen I say to you, unless you eat the flesh of the Son of Man and drink His blood, you will not have life within you." The interpretation of this is quite direct: eat Jesus or go to hell.

That is about it and that is what Catholics do and believe when we receive Christ in the Eucharist. It is us recieving Him body, blood, soul and divinity in what used to be bread.

Of course, many struggle with that belief, even those that claim to be all biblical and what-not. It is a tough sell. Christ knew this, that is why he kept reiterating it over and over again when the people looked at him like he was nuts when he stated we should be eating his body.

At this point, many of his disciples left him. In fact, only twelve stayed and even they didn't know what to think. Peter said so himself when questioned by God. "Lord to who else shall we go?". They knew Him to be the Word, they just didn't understand the saving power of the Eucharist at that point.

Not that we do either, but that is a post for another day...
